Church Leader Encourages Members to Reevaluate Caffeine Use in Observance of Health Code

By Tracie Cudworth | Published April 1, 2026 | 7:37 AM

In a memo published earlier today through the Church’s official newsroom, President Dallin H. Oaks, president of The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints, invited members worldwide to thoughtfully review their personal caffeine consumption as part of their observance of the faith’s health code, commonly referred to as the Word of Wisdom.

The message encouraged members to consider how modern beverages containing significant amounts of caffeine align with the principles taught in Doctrine and Covenants Section 89, which outlines guidance related to physical health and spiritual discipline.

“While the revealed instruction specifically addresses coffee and tea,” the memo stated, “members may find spiritual benefit in thoughtfully evaluating additional sources of caffeine in their daily routines and seeking personal inspiration regarding their use.”

The statement referenced popular drinks such as energy beverages, caffeinated sodas, and other modern stimulant products as examples members might consider when reviewing their habits.

Church leaders emphasized that the reminder was intended to encourage greater mindfulness rather than introduce a formal change to existing standards of worthiness.

“As disciples of Jesus Christ, we continually seek to align our daily choices with principles that promote spiritual clarity, physical health, and reverence for the bodies God has given us,” the memo continued.

Members across social media platforms began discussing the message shortly after its publication, with many noting that the guidance reflects the Church’s long-standing emphasis on personal revelation and thoughtful application of gospel principles.
